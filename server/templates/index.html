SPOT* makes offices more comfortable and office buildings more energy efficient. It has a fan/heater that is controlled by a Raspberry Pi to maintain office workers' personalized comfort. We use a motion sensor to detect occupancy and a temperature sensor to estimate comfort using the PMV equation.

Currently, we are deploying 70 instances of SPOT* on UWaterloo campus. In addition to benefits for the users, this deployment will help us better understand the dynamics of an HVAC system in a building and integrate our system into the building's HVAC system.

To learn more about SPOT*, please click here.

Who is doing this?

The ISS4E Lab at the University of Waterloo started the SPOT project. It is supervised by Prof. Keshav, and currently, Alimohammad Rabbani, Milad Khaki, and Costin Ograda-Bratu are involved in the project. We are open to collaborate with other universities and groups on this project too.

What is this website for?

This website is created primarily for users to control and monitor their SPOT* instances. In addition, it provides admin tools to monitor the deployment and start troubleshooting if something goes wrong. The website also provides a brief description of SPOT* for people who are interested in this area of research.

How can I help?

If you find yourself interested in this project, you can always contact us at cogradab@uwaterloo.ca. We are looking forward to your comments and suggestions and will be more than happy to have you on board if you are interested.
